Hillsong United Tribute Page

Hillsong UNITED

The creators of amazing contemporary christian music

Members of the Hillsong United band - also known as UNITED

Here is a time line of Hillsong United's work:

  • 1998 - Band originally formed out of the Hillsong Church in Australia and was called "Powerhouse." They sing contemporary christian music. Worship leader, Darelene Zschech suggested the group record an album.
  • 1999 - Band rebranded as Hillsong United and EP One was recorded and packaged with the Hillsong Worship annual album. Both achieved Gold sales status in Australia.
  • 2002 - The official UNITED logo was designed by Luke Munns for "To the Ends of the Earth" album. Reuben Morgan stepped down as co-worship leader of the band, and Joel Houston and Marty Sampson led the band together.
  • 2006 - Marty Sampson wrote two more songs, "Devotion" and "Saviour King" for United, married wife Michelle, and stepped down as one of the band's main leaders.
  • 2007 - Devotion" and "Saviour King" was released on album "All of the Above." Album peaked at #1 on the US Christian Albums chart.
  • 2009 - Released album "Across the Earth" in Australia on May 9, which also peaked at #1 on the US Christian Albums chart.
  • 2011 - Released contemporary christian album "Aftermath" on February 15, peaking at #1 on the US Christian Albums chart and #4 on the AUS album chart.
  • 2013 - Contemporary christian album "Zion" released in Australia on February 22, which peaked at #1 on both the US Christian Albums and the AUS Albums charts.
  • 2014 - Hillson United won five Dove Awards.
  • 2015 - Released album "Empires" on May 22. The band was nominated for the second year at American Music Award. They won a Billboard Music Award in the category Top Christian Artist.
  • 2016 - Present band members: Michael Guy Chislett, Matt Crocker, Adam Crosariol, Jonathon Douglass, Jad Gillies, Joel Houston, Simon Kobler, Taya Smith, Benjamin Tennikoff, Dylan Thomas.
